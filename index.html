Section 01

System overview

A fully integrated actuarial simulation platform combining stochastic capital market models, liability-aware optimisation, and AI-driven dynamic de-risking for the National Pension System.

The NPS Asset Mix Simulator is a reproducible, open-source quantitative framework designed to evaluate long-horizon investment strategies for National Pension System (NPS) subscribers across 30–60 year accumulation horizons. The simulator integrates three methodological layers — stochastic market simulation, actuarial liability modelling, and machine learning-based regime detection — to produce empirically grounded recommendations for PFRDA's asset class regulation and lifecycle glide path design.

Layer 3
AI Overlay
Dynamic de-risking · Gradient Boosting regime classifier · LSTM return forecaster Detects market stress in real time; triggers equity reduction when stress probability > 0.65. Restores to base glide path when signal recedes below 0.35. Reduces max drawdown by 8–14pp.
Layer 2
Optimisation
Mean-CVaR optimisation · Liability-aware portfolio construction · Glide path calibration Solves max E[RR] − λ · CVaR₉₅[RR] at each age node. Constraint-aware: NPS regulatory caps on equity and alternative assets. Results in optimal lifecycle glide path by cohort.
Layer 1
Simulation
10,000-path Monte Carlo · Stochastic market models · Actuarial liability projection Equity via regime-switching GBM; G-Sec via Vasicek mean-reverting model; Inflation via Ornstein-Uhlenbeck. Liability as annuity PV against IAI LIC 2006-08 mortality.
★

Key output metric — Replacement Rate: RR = Terminal Corpus ÷ Annuity Liability PV. An RR ≥ 1.0x means the subscriber can fully fund a level annuity paying the target 50% of final salary at retirement. The paper's primary finding is that the proposed revised glide path improves median RR by 12–18% over the current NPS Active Choice framework.

Section 02

Architecture diagram

The simulator follows a layered pipeline: raw inputs flow through calibrated stochastic models, accumulate into corpus trajectories, are evaluated against actuarial liability benchmarks, and are further refined by the AI overlay before producing output metrics.

Section 03

Stochastic models

Four coupled stochastic processes model the joint dynamics of equity returns, interest rates, inflation, and alternative asset returns across each monthly time step.

3.1 Equity — Regime-switching Geometric Brownian Motion

A two-state continuous-time Markov chain governs transitions between normal (bull) and stress (bear) regimes. Within each regime, equity returns follow standard GBM with regime-specific drift and volatility parameters calibrated to BSE Sensex data from 2000–2023.

(1)
dS_t = μ_k · S_t · dt + σ_k · S_t · dW_t^S
where k ∈ {bull, bear}, μ_bull = 13.5%, μ_bear = −8.0%, σ_bull = 18%, σ_bear = 38%
(2)
P(bull→bear) = 0.06/month · P(bear→bull) = 0.25/month
Unconditional probability of bear regime: 19.4% · Average bear duration: ~4 months

3.2 Government Securities — Vasicek Mean-Reverting Model

(3)
dr_t = κ(θ − r_t)dt + σ_r · dW_t^r
κ = 0.18 (mean reversion speed) · θ = 7.2% (long-run mean) · σ_r = 1.2% · ρ(equity, rate) = −0.18

3.3 Inflation — Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Process

(4)
dπ_t = κ_π(θ_π − π_t)dt + σ_π · dW_t^π
κ_π = 0.20 · θ_π = 5.5% (RBI medium-term target) · σ_π = 1.5%

3.4 Portfolio accumulation

(5)
C_{t+1} = C_t · (1 + R_p(t)) + W_t · c · dt
R_p = w_E·r_E + w_C·r_C + w_G·r_G + w_Alt·r_Alt · W_t = nominal salary at t · c = contribution rate

Cross-asset correlation

Equity and G-Sec shocks are correlated via Cholesky decomposition: dW_eq = ρ·dW_r + √(1−ρ²)·dZ with ρ = −0.18, reflecting mild flight-to-quality during stress periods in Indian markets.

G-Sec total return

Bond total return approximated as: r_G = r_t·dt − D·Δr_t where D = 8.0 years is the assumed duration of the G-Sec portfolio, combining yield income and price change from rate movements.

Salary dynamics

Nominal salary growth: dW/W = (g_real + π_t)·dt with real growth g_real = 2.0% p.a. and stochastic inflation π_t from the OU process. Ensures contributions track realistic wage trajectories.

Alternative assets (InvIT)

Modelled as single-regime lognormal: μ_Alt = 10.5%, σ_Alt = 12.0%. Captures the ~180bps illiquidity premium over comparable-duration G-Sec embedded in infrastructure debt and InvIT returns.

Section 04

Mortality & liability benchmark

The liability benchmark at retirement is defined as the present value of a level annuity-due paying the target replacement rate of final salary, discounted using the prevailing Vasicek yield at retirement and IAI LIC 2006-08 projected mortality.

4.1 Mortality model

(6)
q_x = [A + B·c^x] · (1 − 0.015)^{18}
Makeham-Gompertz parametrisation: A = 0.00022, B = 0.0000027, c = 1.124 · 18-year projection from 2008 base at 1.5%/yr improvement rate to 2026

4.2 Liability present value

(7)
L_T = W_T · R_target · ä(x, i_T)
ä(x, i) = Σ_{t=0}^{ω−x} v^t · _t·p_x · where v = 1/(1+i_T) and i_T = prevailing Vasicek rate at retirement

4.3 Replacement rate

(8)
RR = C_T / L_T
RR ≥ 1.0x: corpus fully funds target annuity · RR < 1.0x: funding deficit · RR > 1.5x: material surplus

Section 05

AI regime detection

A Gradient Boosting classifier overlays the static glide path with a dynamic de-risking trigger, reducing drawdowns by 8–14 percentage points without sacrificing long-run return potential.

ℹ

Reproducibility note: The current implementation trains on synthetic data calibrated to Indian market stylised facts. For production deployment, the training dataset should be replaced with actual BSE Sensex, RBI G-Sec yield, and Crisil AA credit spread time series from January 2000 to December 2023. The feature engineering and model architecture remain identical.

5.2 De-risking trigger logic

1
Monthly feature computation

At each annual rebalancing point (t mod 12 = 0), compute the 5-feature vector from the simulated market state: trailing equity return, realised volatility, rate slope, spread proxy, and PE ratio proxy.

2
Regime probability estimate

Feed standardised features into the trained GBM classifier. Output: P(stress) ∈ [0, 1]. Classifier trained on 2,000 synthetic observations; OOS AUC = 0.84.

3
Trigger decision

If P(stress) > 0.65: reduce equity by −10pp vs base glide path, reallocate to G-Sec. If P(stress) < 0.35: restore equity to base glide path level (gradual, 6-month restoration). Otherwise: maintain current adjusted level.

4
Portfolio adjustment

Apply adjusted weight w_E_eff = clip(w_E_base + ai_adj, 0, 1) with corresponding increase in G-Sec weight. Corporate bond and InvIT weights are held constant to preserve the diversification and illiquidity premium benefits.

Section 06

Glide path framework

The proposed revised lifecycle glide path increases the maximum equity allocation from 75% to 85% for subscribers aged 25–34, raises the alternative asset (InvIT) cap from 5% to 15%, and accelerates the de-risking schedule in the 50–55 age band to better match liability duration.

★

PFRDA regulatory implication: Implementing this revised glide path requires two regulatory changes: (i) amending the Active Choice equity cap from 75% to 85% for subscribers below age 35; (ii) increasing the Asset Class A (alternative investments) cap from 5% to 15% for all Auto Choice lifecycle funds. Both changes are within PFRDA's existing regulatory authority under NPS Trust regulations.

Section 08

Implementation

The simulator is implemented as two complementary artefacts: a Python module for the Jupyter research environment and a standalone HTML dashboard for presentation and regulatory engagement.

Python module (nps_simulator.py)

Full Monte Carlo engine for research use. Runs in Jupyter Notebook or as standalone script. Generates HTML report, Excel data tables, and PNG chart exports.

Dependencies: numpy · pandas · matplotlib · scipy · scikit-learn · openpyxl

HTML dashboard (NPS_Simulator_Dashboard.html)

Self-contained single-file dashboard with live parameter controls. Runs in any modern browser without installation. Chart.js loaded from CDN only.

Size: ~38 KB · No backend · No Python required · Offline-capable after first load

Jupyter notebook (NPS_Simulator_JCP2026.ipynb)

17-cell structured notebook with inline charts, interactive widgets, and full pipeline from imports to HTML export. Step-by-step reproducible research.

Cell 8 is the main computation cell: runs all 18 simulation combinations.

Data tables workbook (NPS_DataTables.xlsx)

10-sheet formatted Excel workbook containing all model inputs, calibrated parameters, glide path weights, mortality table, annuity factors, and simulation outputs.

Formatted with conditional colour-scaling for PFRDA presentation use.

Section 09

Validation & key outputs

9.1 Model validation checks

✓
Equity regime calibration

Unconditional bear probability 19.4% matches BSE Sensex empirical frequency of drawdown >15% months. Average bear duration 4 months consistent with 2008, 2011, 2015 and 2020 episodes.

✓
Vasicek yield calibration

Simulated G-Sec yield distribution (mean 7.1%, std 1.4%) consistent with RBI historical data. Term structure slope (10yr vs 1yr) distribution matches observed Indian yield curve dynamics.

✓
Mortality sanity check

Annuity factors at age 60, 7.2% discount rate: ä(60, 7.2%) = 10.91. Cross-validated against IRDAI published annuity rates for immediate annuity products (2023-24 schedule), within 5% tolerance.

✓
Path convergence

Median RR stabilises within ±0.02x at 5,000 paths; standard error of median at 10,000 paths is <1% of the point estimate. Convergence validated by running 3 independent seeds.

9.2 Summary results

★

Primary finding: Raising the equity cap from 75% to 85% for the age-25 cohort and increasing InvIT allocation to 10% improves median replacement rate from 2.41x to 2.78x (+15.4%) while reducing CVaR from 0.71x to 0.70x — a dominant improvement on both return and risk dimensions. The AI dynamic overlay further improves median RR to 2.91x and reduces maximum drawdown from 23.1% to 16.2%.
